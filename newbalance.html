New Balance Gives Back

Year: 2019 | Role: UX/UI Designer | Team: Ana Claudia Henn Alves, Jessica Yang, Shu-Chieh Chuang, Xiaotian Fan.

Overview&Problems
How do we promote New Balance through a digital product?
This is a student project, of which the purpose is to promote New Balance by any type of digital media. During the background reserach on New Balance, our team happens to discover the New Balance Gives Back program that is rarely known by New Balance current and potential customers. New Balance Gives Back is a program where New Balance commited to help deserving organizations raise awareness and funds for their causes. Our team sees the branding value of this program and proposed a mobile application idea to further strengthen NB’s public image as an social responsible company and promote New Balance’s propducts within the application as well.

Built on the foundation of NB Gives Back program,
NB Gives Back app is designed to
promote New Balance’s public image and shoe products by encouraging people to walk/run more with donation credit rewards.

Interface Design
Grphics elements and color palettes based on NB shoes.

To maximize the marketing value of the app, we used New Balance shoes as the core graphic element for the interface design. The challenge progress bar, which is made of a New Balance shoe illustration, varies among different challanges and determines overall color pallete of the app. The idea of providing unique progress bar and color pallete for each challenge is also a way to incentivize users to take more challenges.

Reflection
This is one of the first design sprints I have ever worked on. My gains from the process of collaborating and practicing are fruitful. I would like to share some of my thoughts from a few aspects.

Design Process
Having worked as a landscape designer before, I found UX & UI design process similar to what I have been trained in spatial design. Just like what I did as a landscape designer, we start with researching and understanding the problems. With a good understanding of current situations, we continue with brainstorming and sketching ideas, narrowing down ideas, focusing on fewer ideas and further developing them, making prototype, seeking for feedbacks, and repeat the design and test cycle. The whole process is not new to me at all, thus I was able to follow though the whole design sprint smoothly. Tightly related to people, landscape and UI/UX both concern about how people will be interacting with their products. For landscape, it is how people move and feel in the physical spaces, while for UX/UI of digital products, it is how people going to navigate, perceive and achieve though the digital contents. Although functions and forms of the results of landscape design and UX/UI are different, the cores of their processes are alike. They also share a common value, to serve people and offer them delightful experience.

Interface Design
When it comes to the interface design, I realized that my understanding of how styles of different elements and their arrangement may affect people’s decisions is restricted by my limited experience so far. I am planning to do more case studies of different apps and UI design practices for my future study. Additionally, the job of researching latest UI trend should have been done more comprehensively.

User Test
One thing I found significantly different between digital product design and landscape design is the user test part. In UX/UI design, you can provide users with prototypes that are very alike to the final products in terms of the form and experience. Thus you can receive very effective feedbacks, which are highly applicable to the final product. Whereas in landscape design, it is too costly to provide 1:1 prototype to people. As a result, the discrepancies between design representations and future constructed project in landscape design will cause different degrees of mis-perception and lead to less effective feedback for the design iteration. I am amazed by how designers are able to collect and utilize users’ feedback quickly and effectively at different stages of UX/UI design, which made the whole design approach and final products user-centered. In this project, due to the limited time, we were only able to test our high fidelity prototype with three users. We would have done more user research and test through out our design process, if we had more time.

Collaboration
One of the challenges we have on collaboration is the disagreement within our team on different ideas. I found the vote system mechanism from sprint methods quite effective under many cases. However, discussing and even debating are still necessary during the process. Listening and understanding are critical. Bearing big picture and intention in mind when making group decisions helped us keep our project on track and push it forward efficiently during the two-week duration.
