Project Overview

A note-taking tool for environmental designers tailored to site visiting

To build in the physical world, environmental designers (such as architects and landscape architects) usually need to visit the physical site whenever they start a new project.  They need to jot down all of their observations during the visit for future design decisions. However, taking and organizing site visit-notes is not as easy as it should be.
SiteNote is a geo-tagged note-taking app tailored for environmental designers. It is designed to help them collect, organize and share site visit notes easily and efficiently.

The Challenge

How do we design a note-taking app to help environmental designers with the site visit work?

Interviews

Set aside my own experience & listen to the users

Solving the site visit problem is personal to me because of my own experience as a landscape designer. However, having personal experience with a design problem can be a double-edged sword, as my experience may eclipse what an average user needs. To avoid the “design for myself” situation, I put my own opinions aside, interviewed a few other environmental designers, and listened to their site-visit experiences. To get a more representative sample, I interviewed environmental designers in three different subfields.

Solution

Maps + Geo-tagging  +  Note-taking

A multifunctional geo-tag note-taking product tailored for environmental designers that can be used on different devices.

Principles

During the design process, I realized that I constantly needed to think about users’ use cases to examine my design decisions. To save time and keep focused, I came up with two major principles as the guide for making design decisions in my later design process, which turned out to be efficient. These two principles are based on the user research I did earlier and shown below.

Efficient
Intuitive

The product should be designed to complete users’ needs efficiently. Especially when designers are collecting notes on-site while moving.

The product should be designed simple and standard enough for people with average tech-savviness to use with minimum instructions and training.

Process & Designs

How to present the geographic information effectively

Geographic information is crucial for users throughout the whole process. They use it to navigate during the site visit; they take and organize notes based on it, and they search notes based on it. How the geographic information is presented is critical to the overall user experience.

While seeking a way to combine Map & Note-taking, I found the UI pattern of the whiteboard collaboration tool, Miro, actually suitable for the components of SiteNote. I did a few sketches and came up with V1. The design has evolved during rounds of iterations based on the user testing results. The process is shown below.

What note-taking tools should be included?

To decide what note-taking tools to be included, I did some user interviews and conducted a simple quantitative survey online where I asked designers what types of notes they take during a site visit. A total of 32 surveys were completed.

Types of note taken on site (% of respondents)

The results show that the photo is the most common type of notes people collect on-site. Additionally (not displayed above), it constitutes the most of the notes for any designer. Combined with the interview result, I decided to include five main note-taking tools and make the camera button the most prominent one in the middle. In this way, the camera button can be easily pressed by the left thumb when users are holding a tablet on site. This decision is made based on the principle 1 “Efficient”.

  • Camera button is bigger than other tools’ and placed in the middle to be accessible for frequent uses.

How to make organizing easy and effective?

Organizing the notes is one of the key parts, which determines how effectively notes can be used for future design and presentation. It also influences how notes will be shared.

In the first version, besides the standard search and filter function,  I also designed a collection system (similar to albums) for users to organize notes. During the user test, respondents commented that they see the collection system as a top-down approach for organizing notes and they would only use it when having a clear idea or strong purpose. So they would also like to see a bottom-up approach such as a tagging system to loosely organize notes by attaching relevant labels to individual notes. Therefore, in the later version, I designed a combined system of labels and collections to fulfill the users' needs.

To make organizing easy and effective, I integrated the collection and tag combined system into users’ whole process, from taking notes to utilizing notes.

  • Existing tags are provided to allow users quickly select tags on site.
Edit tags on note detail page (web app)

  • Tags are shown on the notes, making it easy to see and change.
  • Tags are also shown on note info page.

How to make it easy for users to share and collaborate?

In order to make the process of sharing and collaborating more efficient, I tried to make SiteNote as self-contained as possible, which means that users should be able to work together on our platform without relying on other applications or tools. Besides allowing multiple users to view and edit, I also enable users to make comments and sticking them to specific spots on the notes.

  • Comment on any spot of the notes.
  • Comment button.

Qualitative Feedback
All the users I tested with think the app is very easy to use without instructions or training. They think that it is a good solution for their problems and hope to see the app to be built.

To be improved
The current design is still an MVP in terms of the scope. There are many other features to be further developed, especially how to take other types of notes besides photos.

Reflection

Be critical about users’ request
‍Users’ feedback is precious and critical to user experience design. However, I learned deeply in this project that if you try to satisfy every need they have, your project will turn into an uncontrollable monster. It is necessary to define the scope of your work and be critical about users’ requests.
