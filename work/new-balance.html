Project Overview
Nowadays, fashion companies have been exploring new digital technology to promote their brand. One good example is Nike. It has made great achievements in its attempts of creating running apps and gaming websites. New Balance, which has not been very active in this space, is catching up. The purpose of this project is to promote the brand of New Balance through any type of digital media. This is a student sprint design project. We were given two weeks to work on this design sprint.

The Challenge

How do we promote New Balance through a digital product?

Quick Research & Brainstorming

Due to the short project timeline and fast pace, we don’t have much time for detailed research and user interview. We decided to do quick desk researches and brainstorms to find an idea that is manageable within our short schedule.  

During the desk research on New Balance, our team discovered the New Balance Gives Back program. Through this program, New Balance commits to helping deserving organizations raise funds and awareness for their causes.  Unfortunately, the program is hardly known by New Balance's current and potential customers. Our team sees the branding value of this program and proposed a mobile application idea to further strengthen NB’s public image as a socially responsible company. At the same time, it also helps promote New Balance’s products within the application as well.

Reframing Design Challenge

How do we make the New Balance Gives Back program more visible and interactive to NB customers?

Imperfect Process

In this project, due to the tight schedule and communication difficulties within our team, we had to move fast and skipped a few steps that we would have done otherwise. We did not find time for user tests during our low-fi wireframe development, so we ended up doing user tests after we developed our first version of hi-fi mockups. After submitting the school project with my teammate, I revisited the project and did a few more rounds of iterations on the hi-fi mockups and user tests.

Hi-fi Mockup Iterations

I did two rounds of iterations after our team submitted the project. The iterations are done over the hi-fi mockups of our group work. Although iterating drastically over the hi-fi mockups is not the most efficient process, doing it late is still better than doing nothing. My iterations over hi-fi mockups have elevated the design greatly in the end.

    Interface Design

    Inspirations drawn from NB shoe products

    To maximize the marketing value of this app, we used New Balance shoes as the core graphic element for the interface design. The challenge progress bar, which is made of a New Balance shoe illustration, varies on different challenges and determines the color palette of the whole app. The idea of providing a unique progress bar and color palette for each challenge is also a way to incentivize users to take more challenges.

    Results

    Ideal Metrics
    DAU (Daily Active User)

    Usability Test
    All the users we tested were able to complete the tasks smoothly with zero errors. The tasks include view badges collected, view detail information of a badge, share a badge poster to social media and find the rewards.

    Qualitative Feedback
    - Users love the idea that they can contribute to charities by walking more.
    - Users like the final visual design in general. They think it is modern and well-designed.

    To be improved
    - The donation process can be improved to be more easy and fun for users.

    Reflection

    Working at a fast pace and tumbling through an imperfect design process is actually valuable. I learned a lot from the user testing and design iterations. I have also improved my interaction design and prototyping skills tremendously along the way.
